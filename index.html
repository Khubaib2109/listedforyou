How it works

You send the basics. We do the annoying bits.

Listed For You is for people who know their items are worth something, but do not want to spend evenings answering "is this available?"

01

Submit your items

Upload photos, add simple labels like "bike", "mirror" or "console table", and tell us your suburb and minimum price.

02

Approve the plan

We review the item, suggest a sale range, confirm our fee, and check that you are happy before anything goes live.

03

We list and negotiate

We write the description, post the listing, manage messages, screen obvious time-wasters, and bring offers back to you.

04

You get paid

Once the item sells, the buyer pays, the item is collected, and our commission is deducted from the final sale price.

Good fit

Best for items with real resale value that are too good to sit around.

We like selling

  • Sofas, armchairs and dining sets
  • Mirrors, shelves and sideboards
  • Bikes, tools, tech and equipment in good condition
  • Quality homewares, decor and branded pieces
  • Moving, downsizing and spare-room clearouts

We usually avoid

  • Broken, unsafe or heavily damaged items
  • Mattresses and stained soft furnishings
  • High-risk electrical items without clear condition info
  • Jewellery, gold, weapons or regulated products
  • Items worth under $100 unless bundled with other pieces

FAQ

Common questions

Do I pay if nothing sells?

No. The basic model is no sale, no fee. You only pay if the item sells through a buyer introduced by Listed For You.

Do you pick up the item?

Usually the item stays with you until the buyer collects it. Pickup or delivery help can be discussed for larger jobs.

Who decides the final price?

You do. We can recommend pricing and negotiate with buyers, but we check offers with you before accepting.

What happens if a buyer wastes time?

We screen messages, prioritise serious buyers, and avoid sending you every low-effort inquiry.

Can you sell everything in my garage or spare room?

Possibly. We focus on items with enough resale value to justify the time. Low-value items may need to be bundled.

What is the minimum item value?

As a guide, individual items should usually be worth at least $100. Lower-value pieces can still be considered if they form part of a larger bundle.
